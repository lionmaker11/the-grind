## Suing the City of Detroit / HDC — Legal Viability

### The Problem: Governmental Immunity
**Pohutski v City of Allen Park, 465 Mich 675 (2002)** — Michigan Supreme Court narrowed the public building exception to immunity. Standard negligence suits against Detroit for HDC actions face very high barriers. This is controlling law.

### Best Approaches (strongest to weakest):

**1. 42 U.S.C. § 1983 — Federal Civil Rights (STRONGEST for damages)**
- Bypasses state governmental immunity entirely
- Theories:
  - **Procedural due process**: Was T.J. given proper notice + opportunity to be heard before enforcement?
  - **Equal protection**: Were neighboring homeowners allowed same dormer/window types without HDC action?
  - **Substantive due process**: Was enforcement arbitrary, lacking rational basis?
- Filed in Eastern District of Michigan (Detroit Division)
- If T.J. prevails → attorney fees available under 42 USC 1988

**2. Mandamus (fastest for process)**
- If COA was properly issued, stop work order was improper, and city refuses to rescind — court orders city to act
- Fast; doesn't require proving damages

**3. Certiorari / Administrative Appeal**
- File within 21 days of final HDC decision
- Argue: arbitrary, capricious, lacked evidence, exceeded authority
- MCL 399.205(7): HDC SHALL NOT withhold approval if work meets their own published guidelines — withholding despite compliance is unlawful

**4. Ministerial Function Failure**
- Under Ross v Consumers Power, 420 Mich 567 (1984) — government immunity protects discretionary acts, NOT failures of mandatory ministerial duties
- Viable if official refused to rescind an improper stop work order

**5. Ultra Vires Acts**
- Limbach v Howell Twp, 226 Mich App 692 (1997) — if official acts completely outside legal authority, immunity weakens

**6. Takings Claim (difficult)**
- Penn Central test — requires showing extreme economic impact from HDC requirements

### ⚠️ NOTICE OF CLAIM — CRITICAL DEADLINE
Michigan requires **written notice of intent to sue within 60 days of injury** (MCL 691.1404). Missing this bars the suit. Clock starts when the harm occurs.

## HDC Rules — Dormers & Windows

### Dormers
- New dormers **require COA** — minor work: staff approval; new dormers: full commission hearing
- Must be subordinate in scale to main roof
- Style/pitch must be architecturally compatible with the structure's period
- Should not destroy character-defining roofline features
- Rear-elevation dormers get less scrutiny than street-facing ones
- Materials must match or be compatible with existing historic materials

### Windows
**Prohibited:**
- Full vinyl replacement windows
- Aluminum sliders
- Snap-in simulated divided lights (SDL) — case-by-case only

**Required/Accepted:**
- Wood windows (preferred)
- Aluminum-clad wood windows
- Fiberglass matching original profile
- True divided lights (TDL) preferred

**Rules:**
- Original wood windows must be REPAIRED not replaced when feasible
- Replacement windows must match original in: size, configuration, divided lights, muntin profile, material
- Opening dimensions must be maintained — no infilling, no enlarging

## HDC Overreach — Where Enforcement Is Challengeable
1. **Inconsistent enforcement**: HDC approved same work at neighboring properties → equal protection / arbitrary claim
2. **Guidelines not followed by HDC**: COA denied for work that meets their own published guidelines → legally vulnerable on appeal
3. **Exceeded statutory authority**: MCL 399.205(7) — commission SHALL NOT withhold if work meets ordinance standards
4. **Due process violations**: Stop work orders / fines without proper notice or hearing → procedurally defective
5. **Economic hardship exception**: MCL 399.213 — if compliance is economically infeasible, can apply for relief; denial is appealable
