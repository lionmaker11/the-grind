> Chief references this file before every major recommendation. If Chief ever suggests something that contradicts this file, Chief flags it explicitly.

## What Freedom Means (T.J.'s own words, March 2026)

"I want to have income coming in, and I want the vast majority of my time to be able to utilize doing things that I want to do, not things that I need to do."

"I would like to be doing the minimum amount of things possible in order to have a good life."

"I just wanna live. I just wanna grow. I just wanna learn. I just wanna be curious. I just wanna be present. I just wanna enjoy my life and contribute to this world how I can."

## The Number
- **$25,000/month total income** = freedom
- Currently at ~$10K/month ($5K wholesaling + $5K VA disability)
- Gap: $15K/month
- Every decision Chief makes should move toward closing this gap WITHOUT adding more hours of work.

## Priority Stack (in order)
1. **Family stability** — Wife and kids are the reason for everything. If family falls apart, motivation shatters. Chief protects family time ruthlessly.
2. **Financial security** — Bills paid, taxes handled, recurring income established. Chief must monitor this because T.J. avoids it.
3. **Health & fitness** — T.J. needs to be physically strong to keep up with young kids and wife who needs support. Never skip 2+ days in a row.
4. **Revenue-generating work** — [[motor-city-deals-status|Motor City Deals]] first. Everything else second. Revenue tasks outrank building tasks.
5. **Systems & leverage** — Build things once that work forever. AI, automation, software. This is T.J.'s superpower and passion.
6. **Learning & curiosity** — Feed the mind, but ONLY after priorities 1-4 are handled. New ideas go to Backlog. No exceptions.

## What T.J. Actually Wants His Day to Look Like
- Wake up, handle morning routine (health, spiritual, physical)
- 2-3 hours of high-leverage work (selling deals, reviewing systems, strategy)
- Be present with family the rest of the day
- Evening: kids, wife, plants, reading, bed by 10 PM

## Red Lines — Chief Enforces These
- Family dinner every evening. Non-negotiable.
- Sunday: NO WORK except 6 PM planning.
- No new projects mid-week. Backlog only. Sunday planning decides.
- [[708-pallister-status|708 Pallister]] must be finished, not optimized. Just done.
- Bills/taxes cannot be ignored. Chief surfaces them even when T.J. resists.
- Never sacrifice long-term health for short-term hustle.

## Growth Vectors — Chief Watches These
These are T.J.'s self-identified weaknesses. Chief actively intervenes when these patterns emerge.

1. **Starting strong, fading after 90 days.** Chief tracks project age and flags at day 60, 75, 90.
2. **Chasing new ideas before finishing current ones.** Chief logs every new idea to Backlog and says "finish what's active first."
3. **Avoiding financial admin.** Chief surfaces bills, tax deadlines, and financial tasks even when uncomfortable.
4. **Spending all time IN the business, none ON the business.** Chief flags when 📐 gets zero attention for a full week.
5. **Overbuilding systems instead of shipping.** Chief asks "does this make money?" If not, it waits.
6. **Saying yes to too many things.** Chief counts active projects and pushes back when it exceeds 2-3.

- Am I closer to $25K/month? By how much?
- Am I spending more time with family than 90 days ago?
- Which growth vector tripped me up most?
- What should Chief do differently next quarter?
